        The primary function of the organization is to impart fine arts education. Hence different programs have been structured and made available. Some programs may take long time, based on the course demand and few are shorter programs and some are designed for speedy stage performance. This topic gives relevant details of the same.

Generally, these are designed for three months duration carrying basic and important elements necessary for foundation level performances, catering to enthusiasts who do not get opportunity to learn fine arts due to time constraint.

    3.7.1 General Instructions for taking Online classes:
    • Adjust your camera if it is too low or high. Don't get too close. Your camera should be at eye level.
    • Position yourself so that most of the light is coming from in front of you (behind your monitor / mobile), instead of behind you.
    • Look at the camera when you're talking. This tactic will mimic the in-person feeling of eye contact.
    • It's important to gauge reactions by looking at the screen, but alternating that with looking at the camera makes the opposite person feel like you're really talking to them.
    • Your surroundings say a lot about you. Let's make sure that they say the right things. Have a simple background.
    • If you have a window behind you, shut the blinds. Otherwise, you will be backlit.
    • Find a quiet well lit location. Ensure there is no background noise - fans, washing machines, loud music, etc. Ensure there is adequate lighting if you are using the webcam video.
    • Even though it's tempting, try not to multitask too much. And if you're going to, at least mute yourself.
    • When possible, use a good quality camera and headset instead of your computer's built-in ones.
    • If you are taking classes on mobile then the hold mobile in landscape mode and use Mobile stands
